Cookie Policy

This page explains how cookies and similar technologies may be used on the Concrete Service website.

Last updated: June 30, 2026

1. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files that may be stored on your browser or device when you visit a website. Cookies and similar technologies can help a website function, remember preferences, understand how visitors use pages, protect forms from misuse, and support advertising or measurement tools.

Similar technologies may include pixels, tags, local storage, session storage, software development kits, device identifiers, log files, and analytics identifiers.

2. How This Policy Relates to Concrete Service

Concrete Service is a free matching platform that helps homeowners and property owners connect with independent local concrete service providers. Cookies and similar technologies may support the request workflow, help keep forms usable and secure, and help us understand how people navigate service categories such as driveways, repairs, slabs, decorative concrete, walkways, garage floors, and small commercial flatwork.

This Cookie Policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy and Terms & Conditions.

3. Categories of Cookies We May Use

Necessary Cookies

Necessary cookies help the website load, navigate, submit forms, maintain security, remember basic session information, and prevent abuse. Some website features may not work correctly without these cookies.

Preference Cookies

Preference cookies may remember choices such as form progress, browser preferences, region, display settings, or whether you previously dismissed a notice. These cookies help reduce repeated steps during a website visit.

Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies may help us understand page visits, referral sources, device types, service category interest, form drop-off, and website performance. This information may be used to improve content, navigation, request forms, and matching workflow.

Security and Fraud Prevention Technologies

Security technologies may help detect spam, bots, automated abuse, suspicious activity, repeated submissions, or technical attacks. These tools may collect IP address, browser details, interaction signals, or similar technical information.

Advertising and Measurement Cookies

Where used, advertising or measurement cookies may help understand whether visitors found Concrete Service through search, ads, partner links, or other campaigns. They may also help measure the effectiveness of service category pages or request forms.

4. Cookies in the Matching Request Process

When you submit or interact with a matching request, cookies and similar technologies may help:

  • Keep the request form functioning correctly.
  • Reduce duplicate or spam submissions.
  • Understand which service category or page led to a request.
  • Measure form usability and improve future request flows.
  • Support security logs and troubleshooting if a submission fails.

Cookies are not a substitute for the project information you submit directly. Project details, photos, measurements, contact information, and location information are handled as described in the Privacy Policy.

5. First-Party and Third-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are placed by or for Concrete Service. Third-party cookies are placed by outside companies that provide services such as analytics, hosting, security, form protection, embedded scripts, advertising measurement, maps, fonts, or other website functionality.

Third-party tools may process information according to their own policies. Concrete Service does not control every technical choice made by third-party providers, browsers, devices, or networks.

6. Examples of Information Cookies May Collect

Depending on the tools used, cookies and similar technologies may collect or infer:

  • IP address and approximate location.
  • Browser, device, operating system, and screen size.
  • Pages viewed, links clicked, referring page, and visit time.
  • Whether a visitor returned to the website.
  • General interaction with forms, buttons, navigation, or service pages.
  • Security signals used to identify spam, bots, or suspicious activity.

7. How Long Cookies Last

Session cookies usually expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies may remain for a set period or until you delete them. The exact duration depends on the cookie, browser settings, and the third-party tool involved.

Server logs and analytics records may be retained separately from browser cookies for operational, security, reporting, or legal purposes.

8. Your Cookie Controls

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block cookies, delete existing cookies, limit third-party cookies, clear site data, or receive alerts when cookies are being used. If you block or delete cookies, some parts of the website may not function as intended.

You may also be able to limit tracking through device settings, browser privacy tools, ad settings, private browsing modes, or third-party opt-out tools. The availability and effectiveness of these controls depends on your device, browser, region, and the third-party tools involved.

9. Do Not Track and Global Privacy Signals

Some browsers or extensions offer Do Not Track or global privacy signals. The website may not respond to every signal in the same way because standards and legal requirements vary. Where a legally recognized browser-based opt-out signal applies and is technically detectable, we will handle it as required by applicable law.

10. Consent and Notice

Depending on your location and applicable law, the website may present cookie notices, consent tools, or preference controls. If such controls are available, your choices may be stored using a cookie or similar technology so the website can remember them.

If no specific consent tool is shown, you can still use browser settings to manage cookies and contact us with questions.

11. Cookies and Independent Providers

Independent providers who receive your request may use their own websites, forms, communication tools, analytics, advertising systems, or cookies. Concrete Service is not responsible for the cookie practices of independent providers. You should review a provider's own policies if you visit that provider's website or use that provider's tools.

12. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in website technology, analytics, legal requirements, or matching operations. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised date.
