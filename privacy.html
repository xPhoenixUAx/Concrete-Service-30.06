Privacy Policy

This policy explains how Concrete Service may collect, use, share, and retain information submitted through this free provider matching website.

Last updated: June 30, 2026

1. Overview of This Privacy Policy

Concrete Service is a free homeowner matching platform. We help people submit concrete project information and may route that information to independent local concrete service providers who may contact the homeowner about inspection, estimates, scheduling, or related project questions.

This Privacy Policy describes the information we may collect when you visit the website, submit a request, contact us, use a form, click a link, or otherwise interact with Concrete Service. It also explains how information may be shared with independent providers and service vendors that help operate the website.

Concrete Service is not a concrete contractor, does not employ the providers who may contact you, and does not control the privacy practices of independent providers once information is shared with them for matching purposes.

2. Information You Provide Directly

When you submit a concrete service request, contact us, or communicate with us, we may collect information that you choose to provide, including:

  • Name, email address, phone number, and preferred contact method.
  • Property city, ZIP code, service area, or address details needed to route a request.
  • Concrete service category, such as driveways, slabs, repairs, resurfacing, stamped concrete, walkways, garage floors, or small commercial flatwork.
  • Project descriptions, approximate dimensions, surface condition, drainage concerns, access notes, urgency, preferred timing, budget context if provided, and other project details.
  • Photos, files, or other materials you upload or send to help describe the project.
  • Messages, call notes, form submissions, support requests, and related communication records.
  • Information about whether you are a homeowner, property manager, business owner, tenant, or other requester, if you provide that context.

3. Information Collected Automatically

When you visit the website, certain technical information may be collected automatically by the website, hosting provider, analytics tools, security tools, or similar technologies. This may include:

  • IP address, approximate location derived from IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, screen size, and language settings.
  • Pages viewed, links clicked, referring page, date and time of visit, time spent on pages, and interaction events.
  • Cookie identifiers, session identifiers, analytics identifiers, and similar technology identifiers.
  • Form interaction data used to detect spam, abuse, fraud, bot activity, or security issues.

For more information about cookies and similar technologies, please review our Cookie Policy.

4. How We Use Information

We may use information for the following purposes:

  • To receive, organize, review, and respond to concrete project requests.
  • To help route a request to independent local providers who may handle the requested type of concrete work.
  • To allow providers to contact you about estimates, availability, site visits, project scope, documentation, or scheduling.
  • To communicate with you about your inquiry, request status, support questions, or website updates.
  • To improve website functionality, content, user experience, form design, service categories, and matching workflow.
  • To monitor website performance, troubleshoot errors, and protect against misuse, spam, fraud, unauthorized activity, or security incidents.
  • To maintain internal records, comply with applicable law, enforce our Terms & Conditions, or respond to lawful requests.
  • To evaluate aggregate trends, such as which service categories are requested most often, without necessarily identifying a specific person.

5. Sharing With Independent Providers

When you submit a request for matching, you authorize Concrete Service to share relevant request information with independent local providers, contractors, or service businesses that may be able to respond to your project. The information shared may include your contact information, location/service area, project category, project description, photos, timing, and other details needed for the provider to evaluate the request.

Providers are independent businesses. They are not employees, agents, partners, or representatives of Concrete Service. Providers may have their own privacy practices, terms, estimates, contracts, payment requirements, insurance coverage, licensing obligations, and communication methods. You should review any provider's privacy practices directly if you have questions about how that provider handles your information.

6. Sharing With Service Vendors

We may share information with vendors that help us operate, secure, analyze, host, maintain, or improve the website and matching workflow. These may include hosting providers, analytics providers, form tools, email or messaging tools, spam prevention services, customer support tools, data storage services, and technical contractors.

These vendors are expected to use information only as needed to provide services to Concrete Service, subject to their own contractual, technical, and legal obligations.

7. Business Transfers and Legal Requirements

Information may be disclosed or transferred if Concrete Service is involved in a merger, acquisition, reorganization, sale of assets, financing, bankruptcy, or similar business transaction. Information may also be disclosed if we believe disclosure is necessary to comply with law, respond to legal process, protect rights or safety, investigate suspected misuse, enforce website terms, or prevent fraud or security issues.

8. Marketing and Follow-Up Communications

We may use your contact information to respond to your request, follow up about your inquiry, confirm project details, send service-related messages, or communicate about matching opportunities. Where permitted, we or independent providers may contact you by phone, text, email, or other contact methods you provide.

You may ask us to stop non-essential communications by contacting us. Transactional or administrative communications may still be sent when necessary to respond to your request, maintain records, or comply with obligations.

9. Photos and Project Materials

If you submit photos, videos, documents, drawings, measurements, or other project materials, you should avoid including sensitive personal information that is not needed for matching. For example, avoid showing private documents, security codes, license plates, children, unrelated people, valuables, or areas of the property that are not relevant to the concrete project.

Project materials may be shared with independent providers as part of request routing. They may also be retained for operational records, quality review, fraud prevention, or dispute documentation.

10. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including request handling, provider routing, customer support, recordkeeping, security, fraud prevention, legal compliance, and business operations.

Retention periods may vary depending on the type of information, the nature of the request, whether a provider connection occurred, whether there is an unresolved issue, and applicable legal or operational needs. We may retain limited records even after deletion requests where needed for legal, security, accounting, anti-fraud, or dispute purposes.

11. Data Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect information. However, no website, transmission method, storage system, or electronic communication is completely secure. You submit information at your own discretion and should avoid sending unnecessary sensitive information.

General data security principles reflected in business guidance from regulators include knowing what information is collected, limiting unnecessary collection, protecting retained information, disposing of information that is no longer needed, and planning for security incidents. We aim to use practices consistent with those principles for the type and scale of this website.

12. Your Choices and Requests

Depending on where you live and which laws apply, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, portability, restriction, objection, or information about certain sharing practices. You may contact us at to make a request.

We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request. We may deny or limit requests where permitted by law, including when information is needed to complete a transaction, maintain security, prevent fraud, comply with law, protect legal rights, or maintain internal records.

13. State Privacy Rights

Some U.S. state privacy laws provide additional rights to residents, such as the right to know categories of personal information collected, sources of information, purposes of use, categories of third parties receiving information, and rights to delete or correct certain information. If such laws apply to Concrete Service and to your request, we will respond as required.

Concrete Service does not intend to collect sensitive personal information unless you voluntarily include it in a request. Please do not submit sensitive information unless it is necessary for your inquiry.

14. Children

This website is intended for adults seeking concrete service provider matching. It is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child provided personal information, please contact us so we can review and address the issue.

15. Third-Party Links

The website may link to third-party websites, provider websites, maps, tools, or resources. We are not responsible for the privacy practices, security, accuracy, or content of third-party websites or independent providers.

16. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be posted on this page with a revised date. If changes materially affect how information is collected, used, or shared, we may provide additional notice where appropriate.
