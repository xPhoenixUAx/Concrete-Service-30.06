Grouped services

Concrete categories with the common project points inside each one.

New concrete

Pour, replace, and build new surfaces

Best for projects where the concrete surface is being created from scratch or fully removed and replaced.

  • Driveway installation, replacement, widening, aprons, and parking pads.
  • Garage slabs, shed pads, equipment pads, footings, and foundation-adjacent flatwork.
  • Walkways, steps, stoops, landings, and entry paths with new forms and finish planning.
  • Details to prepare: dimensions, access, slope, drainage, load, demolition needs, and finish preference.
Concrete Driveways Slabs & Foundations Sidewalks, Steps & Walkways Garage Floors & Interior Concrete
Existing surfaces

Repair, restore, and resurface

For concrete that is cracked, pitted, spalling, uneven, worn, stained, or showing water-related damage.

  • Crack filling, patching, spall repair, overlays, and surface renewal.
  • Trip hazard review for walks, porches, steps, patios, and garage floors.
  • Details to prepare: close photos, damage age, pooling water, prior coatings, and movement concerns.
Concrete Repair & Resurfacing Garage Floor Repair Walkway & Step Repair
Finish upgrades

Decorative patios, texture, and color

For homeowner projects where appearance, pattern, color, and maintenance are part of the decision.

  • Stamped patios, textured walkways, colored concrete, borders, and outdoor living areas.
  • Sealing, resealing, slip-resistance discussion, and sample-board review.
  • Details to prepare: inspiration photos, preferred tones, wet-area use, and resealing expectations.
Stamped & Decorative Concrete Decorative Driveway Options Decorative Walkways
Business property

Small commercial and access flatwork

For storefronts, small business properties, shared access routes, equipment pads, and public-facing surfaces.

  • Storefront walks, pads, ramps, curbs, loading areas, dumpster pads, and concrete sections.
  • Work-window planning, barricades, tenant access, curing time, and reopening expectations.
  • Details to prepare: property access, business hours, photos, safety concerns, and insurance needs.
Commercial Concrete Flatwork Equipment Pads & Slabs Public-Facing Walkways

Use the condition of the surface to choose the first conversation.

A project may touch more than one category. Start with the most urgent part: new placement, visible damage, finish appearance, or access for a business property.

Before matching

A clearer request helps providers respond with fewer guesses.

Photos and measurements are not busywork. They help independent providers understand access, condition, surface use, and whether the project should be estimated as repair, replacement, decorative work, or commercial flatwork.

PhotosWide shots, close damage shots, access routes, steps, slopes, and drainage areas.
MeasurementsApproximate length, width, depth concerns, step count, or square footage.
ContextVehicle load, coating plans, finish preference, timing needs, and known restrictions.
